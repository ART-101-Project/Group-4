What is A.I.?

Artificial intelligence is technology that allows computers and machines to engage in human intelligence in order to accomplish problem solving. Emerging as a field of computer science, AI seeks to process data information while recognizing patterns and making predictions/decisions modeled after human judgment. Linked to machine and deep learning, artificial intelligence “learns” from existing information that is programmed within the system. This allows the machine to facilitate cognitive functions that are usually associated with human minds and subsequent thinking.

The 7 Main Types of Artifical Intelligence

The Creation Process

Narrow AI or Also Known As ANI Narrow AI is any kind of artificial intelligence system and/or form of AI technology that's sole purpose is to complete a set of very specific actions or basic commands. While ANI's are created with the intended goal in mind of serving and exceling in a cognitive capability, this particular sub-category of AI technologies still aren't able to independently learn new skills that it's not already programmed and/or wasn't intially designed to learn. These kind of technologies are developed by combining a subfield of artifical intelligence called "machine learning" with neural network algorithms to fucntion properly and accomplish the specificed tasks it's programmed to complete. Through machine learning, AI systems are provided with the ability to process data and statistics, while simultaneously applying problem-solving strategies that are similar to that of strategies used by actual people, like that of trail and error, to improve it's own processes and "think" at faster rates. Examples of such A.I. include image recognition software, self-driving cars, and AI virtual assistants.

Artifical Genral Intelligence (AGI) Artificial General Intelligence and/or general AI is any form of artificial intelligence that's capacity for thinking, learning, and preforming more closely resembles that of a real person, as it's able to preform more divesive and detailed directions/tasks. While AGI's are still a working progress, the fundamental framework for this type of AI may be constructed from technologies such as supercomputers, quantum hardware and generative AI models like that of ChatGPT.

Artificial Superintelligence Artificial Superintelligence and/or super AI is more of a figment of the imgaination and is showcased through numerous science fiction movies like that of The Termninator and Machina. It's the infamously known theroy that artificial intelligence will become significantly more knowledgable and efficient than humans, eventually becoming the backbone of another sub-genre of AI called self-aware AI and other individualistic, robotic projects. As CEO of the AI writing company, Jasper, Dave Rogenmoser once said himself,"Artificial superintelligence will become by far the most capable forms of intelligence on earth.... It will have the intelligence of human beings and will be exceedingly better at everything that we do."

Reactive Machine AI While reactive machines are the most fundamental type of artificial intelligence, they are also greatly limited in their ability to store memory, learn from past experiences and thus, modify their own functionality through experiences. They're msotly useful for preforming basic autonomous functions, such as filtering spam from email inbox's and/or recommending items based on personal shopping history. An example of sucb would be that of the reactive machine, Deep Blue, whihc in 1997 was able to read realtime cues during a chess match against grandmaster, Garry Kasparov and actually beat him.

Limited Memory AI Limited Memory AI is any kind of artificial intelligence tool that can make predictions from previously retrieved and stored data. It's able to preform tasks based on the knowledge that it acquries from the data it's saved up and applies a subset of machine learning called "Deep Learning" that resembles the function of nenrons in the human brain. From such, different variations of AI like Limited Memory AI are able to gather information from experiences and "learn" ways to enahnce the accruacy of themselves gradually. The limited memory model represents a majority of AI applicants and can be employed in a variety settings that range from chatbots to self-driving cars. In fact, examples of this include chatbots and self-driving cars.
